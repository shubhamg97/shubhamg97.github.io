RESEARCH INTERN
Qatar Computing Research Institute

As a research intern with the Cyber Security division of QCRI, my topic of study is the "Real Time Detection and Identification of Phishing URLs and Domains". The focus of the study is on combosquatting and typosquatting domains and how they can be quickly detected and identified as benign or malicious using machine learning algorithms. In the first stage, I have taken websites from Alexa's top 1 million sites and then categorized and itemized them. This data is then used to query Farsight's PDNS database to gather more information about a website and it's A records. All this data is stored in several MongoDB collections, each with a purpose, to help in collecting and building information on identifying malicious domains faster and more accurately. In the next stage, I will be using the data we've collected as well as the data from the end-user to actually identify whether or not the domain is malicious. Initially, the identifiation will be slow since we will be building information on the dataset, but eventually this process will be, for all practical purposes, instantaneous. This stage is where I designed and used machine learning algorithms so that once the initial information is collected, the program will begin to learn by itself about the legitimacy of a domain.
